# Help-Desk / Social Worker — Quick Guide (1 page)

**Your job:** get every waiting patient onto the pre-consultation form on their phone,
and help those who can't do it alone. The form fills the wait time so the doctor gets
a ready summary. **It never replaces the doctor.**

---

## The one thing patients do
Point their phone camera at the **OPD poster QR code** → the form opens in their browser.
No app to install. Works in **English / हिंदी / తెలుగు** (patient picks first).

## Walk a patient through it (≈5–8 min)
1. **Language** — let them choose; the whole form + read-aloud follows it.
2. **Phone number** → they get a **6-digit OTP by SMS** → type it in.
3. **Their details** — name, age, gender.
4. **Department** — tap the department they've come for. They can also pick a
   **preferred doctor** if they have one (optional).
5. **Consent** — read it aloud if needed; they must agree before anything is stored.
6. **Documents** *(optional)* — take a photo of old prescriptions / lab reports.
   Tell them to hold the paper flat, good light, whole page in frame.
7. **Questions** — a few symptom questions ("Question 3/7" shows progress). They can
   **type, tap, or speak** the answer (mic button). Assisted mode reads questions aloud.
8. **Review & submit** — a short confirmation screen; they tap **Submit** to finish.
   (Vitals like BP/weight/SpO₂ are **not** entered by the patient here — a nurse/staff member
   records them separately for departments that collect them.)
9. **Done** — a **token number** appears (e.g. `OPD-007`). Tell them to **watch the
   waiting-room screen** for their token and keep the phone page open.

## Who needs your help
- **No smartphone / low literacy / elderly** → use the **help-desk tablet**: do the form
  *with* them, read questions aloud, enter answers they tell you.
- **Didn't get the OTP** → check the number, wait 30s, resend once. Poor signal? Move
  near a window or use the help-desk tablet on wifi.
- **Camera won't scan** → type the short URL on the poster into the browser instead.

## Do / Don't
✅ Reassure them it's **free, optional, and private** — only their care team sees it.
✅ Let them **skip** any question they're unsure about.
✅ Encourage the **photo of old reports** — it saves the doctor time.
❌ Don't diagnose or interpret the questions for them — just read/translate.
❌ Don't enter answers they didn't give. Don't fill in someone else's phone number.
❌ Don't share a patient's details with anyone but the care team.

## If the system is down
Switch to the **paper intake slip** (see the downtime SOP by the desk) and carry on —
the queue still runs on paper tokens. Tell the shift lead.
