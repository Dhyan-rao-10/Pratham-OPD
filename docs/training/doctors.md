# Doctor — Quick Guide (1 page)

**What this gives you:** before the patient walks in, an **AI-drafted pre-consultation
summary** from their answers, uploaded reports, and vitals — so you start informed.
**It is decision-support only. You decide. Always verify.**

---

## Log in
Go to the **doctor dashboard** → enter your **phone + PIN**.
**First login: change the default PIN** (top-right / profile → Change PIN). Never share it.

## Your screen
- **Queue** — patients waiting in your department, **urgent (RED) first**, then arrival order.
- **Consulting** — the patient you've opened (only one at a time).
- **Consulted** — patients you've finished today.

## Seeing a patient
1. **Open** the patient card → this **locks** them to you (others see "being consulted").
2. Read the **Summary** tab: chief complaint, history, current meds, allergies, flags.
   - **🚨 RED / ⚠ AMBER** flags = the AI thinks it may be urgent. **You confirm.**
   - **Previous visits** are grouped under the patient — including visits in **other
     departments** — so you see their history even after a reassignment.
3. **Documents** — tap to view the photos the patient uploaded; the AI's extracted
   meds/labs are shown next to the image. **Check against the actual image.**
4. **Listen** — you can play back the patient's **voice answers** in their own words.

## Prescribe & finish
- **Prescribe** tab — add drugs; the system flags possible **interactions** (advisory —
  use your judgement). You can prescribe **advice only, no medication**.
- **Scribe** *(optional)* — record the consultation for an AI draft note; **review before saving**.
- **Save & Generate QR** — completes the visit, prints the patient's slip, releases the lock.
- **Reassign** — send the patient to another doctor/department if needed (history follows them).

## Rate the summary (30 seconds, important)
Mark each report **Accurate / Inaccurate**. If it's wrong, **edit the text** before saving.
This is how we measure and improve the AI — please do it every time.

## Golden safety rules
✅ The AI **drafts**; the diagnosis and prescription are **yours**.
✅ **Verify** extracted medicines, doses, and lab values against the source image.
✅ Treat triage flags as a **prompt to look**, not a conclusion.
❌ Never auto-accept an AI summary or interaction note without reading it.
❌ Never rely on it for an emergency — examine the patient.

## Escalating a RED / emergency
A RED-triage patient sends a **nursing alert**. If a patient in front of you is
deteriorating, follow the **normal hospital emergency protocol immediately** — do not
wait on the system.
